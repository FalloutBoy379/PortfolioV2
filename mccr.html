Motion Capture Calibration Robot

Automating OptiTrack calibration with a cable-suspended parallel robot

Abstract

This project implements precise path planning and real-time control for a custom CSPR to calibrate a 6×9 m motion-capture space. Leveraging NEMA 17 motors, AS5600 encoders, Dyneema SK99 cables, and an ESP32S3-based controller, the system executes closed-loop trajectories while avoiding obstacles via RRT*. Detailed methodologies and experimental validation are in the technical report.

1. Introduction

Cable-driven parallel robots provide large workspaces and high payload-to-weight ratios. This CSPR positions a reflective pendant at known waypoints within an OptiTrack MoCap room for calibration routines. Challenges included maintaining cable tension, real-time IK computation, and collision avoidance amid support structures.

2. Mechanical Design

2.1 Drum & Cable Interface

Custom PLA “squircle” drum (40 mm Ø, 1.32 mm groove) printed at 0.1 mm, with acrylic endplates and nylon standoffs for slippage-free winding.

2.2 Cable Selection

Dyneema SK99 (1.3 mm) demonstrated <0.5% elongation under 10 N loads and <±2 mm deviation across the workspace. Dyneema Specs.

2.1.1 Drum Slot Evolution

The groove profile went through multiple iterations—square, circular, and hybrid “squircle”—to perfectly cradle the Dyneema rope without deforming its cross-section under load.

2.3 Room Corner Setup

This image shows one corner of the OptiTrack MoCap room with the sample mass (reflective pendant) attached via the cable system, illustrating the initial calibration position.

3. Electronics & Firmware

3.1 AS5600 Interface

Custom PCB with PH-4 connectors, pull-ups, and decoupling for stable 1 kHz encoder sampling.

3.2 Main Control Board

  • ESP32S3 (FreeRTOS, dual-core)
  • TB6600 drivers (1/64 micro-stepping, 1.5 A RMS)
  • Web dashboard: live pendant position & telemetry

3.3 Interactive PCB Model

Rotate, zoom, and inspect the PCB right in your browser.

4. Control & Kinematics

4.1 Inverse Kinematics & PID

Using NatNet streaming, target coordinates feed IK module to compute cable lengths and velocities. A PID loop (50 ms) corrects length errors for smooth tracking.

4.2 RRT* Path Planning

RRT* generates collision-free trajectories around obstacles (sphere radius rj). Once the goal threshold (50 mm) is met, the optimized path is executed.

RRT* planner in action: path from start to goal around a spherical obstacle.

5. Simulator Visualization

A desktop-based simulator replicates the workspace and allows for trajectory planning tests without hardware, showing cable attachment points, obstacles, and target waypoints.

6. Experimental Results

Square-path tests (2 m side) yielded <35 mm RMS error under dynamic loads up to 10 N.
